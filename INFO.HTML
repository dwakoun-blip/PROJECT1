Amber fort

Amber Fort, also called Amer Fort, is a grand historical fort built on a hill in Amer, about 11 km from Jaipur in Rajasthan. The construction of the fort was started by Raja Man Singh I in 1592, one of the most trusted generals of Mughal Emperor Akbar. Later, Mirza Raja Jai Singh and Maharaja Sawai Jai Singh II expanded and improved the fort. Made with red sandstone and white marble, the fort beautifully mixes Rajput and Mughal architecture. Inside the fort are many iconic sections like Diwan-i-Aam, Diwan-i-Khas, the beautiful Ganesh Pol gate and the famous Sheesh Mahal (Mirror Palace), where small mirror pieces reflect light like stars. Before Jaipur city was established, Amber Fort served as the main royal residence of the Kachwaha Rajput rulers. Today, Amber Fort is a UNESCO World Heritage site, known worldwide for its history, architecture, light and sound show, and elephant rides, making it a major tourist attraction.

hawa mahal

Hawa Mahal, also known as the “Palace of Winds”, is one of the most iconic monuments in Jaipur, Rajasthan. It was built in 1799 by Maharaja Sawai Pratap Singh, and designed by the architect Lal Chand Ustad. Made of red and pink sandstone, it reflects the beautiful Rajput architectural style. The structure has five floors and around 953 small windows called “jharokhas”. These windows allow cool breeze to pass inside, which made the palace comfortable during hot summers. The main purpose of Hawa Mahal was to allow royal women to observe street festivals, markets and everyday life from behind the windows without being seen, because during that time, women followed the purdah system. The front of the palace looks like a honeycomb of a beehive, which makes it very unique. Today, Hawa Mahal is one of the most popular tourist attractions of Jaipur and is an important symbol of the Pink City’s culture and heritage.

Jal mahal

Jal Mahal, meaning “Water Palace”, is one of the most beautiful historical monuments of Jaipur, Rajasthan. It is located in the middle of Man Sagar Lake, between Jaipur and Amer. The palace was originally built in the 18th century by Maharaja Madho Singh I as a summer retreat and a place for royal duck-hunting parties. Jal Mahal is made using Rajput style of architecture with some Mughal influences. The palace looks like it is floating on water, but actually it has five floors, out of which four floors remain underwater when the lake is full, and only the top floor remains visible. Jal Mahal cannot be visited from the inside today, but visitors can enjoy its stunning view from the banks of the lake. At night, when the palace lights reflect on the water, the view becomes even more magical. Jal Mahal is one of Jaipur’s most photographed tourist attractions.

city palace

City Palace is one of the most important historical landmarks in Jaipur, Rajasthan. It is located in the heart of the Pink City and was built in the early 18th century by Maharaja Sawai Jai Singh II, the founder of Jaipur. The palace is a beautiful blend of Rajput, Mughal and European architectural styles. The complex includes several buildings, courtyards, gardens, temples and museums. Some famous parts of the City Palace are the Chandra Mahal, Mubarak Mahal, Diwan-i-Aam, Diwan-i-Khas, Peacock Gate and the beautiful armoury section which displays royal weapons, swords and shields. Chandra Mahal is still partly used as the royal residence by the Jaipur royal family. The City Palace also preserves many royal costumes, jewellery, paintings and manuscripts which show the rich culture and history of Rajput rulers. Today, City Palace is a major tourist attraction that tells the glorious story of Jaipur’s royal heritage.

Nahargarh Fort

Nahargarh Fort is a majestic hilltop fort located on the Aravalli hills, overlooking Jaipur city in Rajasthan. It was originally built in 1734 by Maharaja Sawai Jai Singh II, the founder of Jaipur, as a strong defensive structure to protect the city. Later, more palaces and rooms were added during the rule of Maharaja Sawai Ram Singh and Maharaja Sawai Madho Singh. The fort was also connected with Amer Fort and Jaigarh Fort for a stronger defense system. One of the interesting legends says that the fort is named after the spirit of Nahar Singh Bhomia, who was believed to protect the place. Nahargarh Fort offers a beautiful view of the entire Jaipur city, especially at sunset, making it a popular tourist spot. Inside the fort, “Madhavendra Bhawan” with twelve interconnected suites is the main attraction. In the past, the fort was used as a retreat for the royal family. Today, Nahargarh Fort stands as an important symbol of Jaipur’s history and architecture.

Jantar Mantar

Jantar Mantar is a famous astronomical observatory located in Jaipur, Rajasthan. It was built by Maharaja Sawai Jai Singh II in the early 18th century. Jai Singh II was very interested in astronomy and wanted to improve the accuracy of astronomical calculations. Jantar Mantar consists of 19 large instruments made of stone and marble. These instruments were used to measure time, predict eclipses, study the movement of planets and stars, and observe astronomical positions. The most famous instrument here is the Samrat Yantra, which is the world’s largest stone sundial. It can measure time with remarkable accuracy. Other instruments include the Jai Prakash Yantra, Ram Yantra and Misra Yantra. The observatory shows the scientific knowledge and intelligence of ancient India. In 2010, Jantar Mantar Jaipur was declared a UNESCO World Heritage site. Today it is an important tourist attraction and a symbol of India’s scientific heritage.
