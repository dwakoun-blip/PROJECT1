Dal Bhati Churma

**Dal Baati Churma** is one of the most iconic and beloved dishes of Jaipur and the wider Rajasthani cuisine. It is a wholesome combination of three components: **baati**, **dal**, and **churma**. Baati are round, hard wheat rolls baked or roasted to perfection, giving them a crisp exterior and soft interior. These are served with **dal**, typically made from lentils and flavored with aromatic spices, creating a savory and comforting taste. Churma, the sweet element of the dish, is made by crushing baked baati and mixing it with ghee and jaggery or sugar, offering a delightful contrast to the savory dal and baati. This dish not only represents the culinary richness of Rajasthan but also reflects the ingenuity of using simple ingredients to create a filling and flavorsome meal. Dal Baati Churma is often enjoyed during festivals and special occasions, making it a must-try for anyone visiting Jaipur.

Ghevar

**Ghevar** is a traditional Rajasthani sweet that is especially popular in Jaipur during festivals like **Teej, Raksha Bandhan, and Diwali**. It is a disc-shaped dessert made from **refined flour (maida), ghee, and sugar syrup**, often topped with **chopped nuts or saffron**. Ghevar has a crispy, lacy texture on the outside while being soft and rich inside, offering a perfect balance of sweetness and flavor. It comes in different varieties, such as **plain, mawa, or malai ghevar**, each with its unique taste. This delicacy is not just a treat for the palate but also a part of Rajasthan’s cultural heritage, often exchanged as gifts during celebrations. Enjoyed with family and friends, Ghevar symbolizes the festive spirit of Jaipur and is a must-try for anyone visiting the city.

Pyaaz ki Kachor

**Pyaz ki Kachori** is a famous and delicious snack from Jaipur, loved by locals and tourists alike. It is a **deep-fried pastry stuffed with a spicy onion filling**, often flavored with cumin seeds, dry mango powder, and other traditional Rajasthani spices. The outer layer is crisp and golden, while the inside is soft and bursting with flavorful onions, making it a perfect combination of crunch and spice. Pyaz ki Kachori is usually served with **tangy green chutney or sweet tamarind chutney**, enhancing its taste. It is a popular street food, commonly enjoyed as a **breakfast item or evening snack with tea**. This savory delicacy reflects Jaipur’s rich culinary heritage and is a must-try for anyone exploring the city’s traditional flavors.

Mawa Kachori

**Mawa Kachori** is a famous sweet delicacy from Jaipur, known for its rich and indulgent taste. Unlike the savory kachoris, mawa kachori is **filled with mawa (khoya), sugar, and dry fruits**, giving it a sweet and creamy center. The outer layer is a crisp, golden pastry that perfectly complements the soft, rich filling inside. Often garnished with **saffron, cardamom, and chopped nuts**, it is both flavorful and visually appealing. Mawa Kachori is a popular dessert during **festivals, special occasions, and celebrations** in Rajasthan. It is commonly found in sweet shops across Jaipur and is enjoyed as a treat with family or guests. This delicacy beautifully represents the **rich culinary tradition of Jaipur**, combining sweetness, texture, and aromatic flavors in every bite.
